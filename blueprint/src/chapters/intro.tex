\chapter{Introduction}

The ISAR (Invariant Scalable Fractal Addressable Representations) kernel provides a unified, substrate-independent framework that bridges discrete combinatory logic (symbolic rewriting) and continuous analysis (neural networks and physical systems). In this blueprint, we formalize the continuous universal approximation and representation space of the ISAR kernel, showing that all continuous functions $\mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{R}^k$ are uniquely represented as limit addresses in the completion of the quotient address space.
